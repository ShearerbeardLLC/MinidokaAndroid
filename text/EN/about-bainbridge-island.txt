The Japanese American Exclusion Memorial on Bainbridge Island, Washington, is part of Minidoka National Historic Site. It honors the first Nikkei forced from their homes under Executive Order 9066. For visiting information or tours, contact the Bainbridge Island Historical Society using the information below. The memorial is a joint effort of the historical society and Bainbridge Island Exclusion Memorial Association, Bainbridge Island Japanese American Committee, and City of Bainbridge Island. Klondike Gold Rush National Historic Site in Seattle, Washington, provides National Park Service administrative assistance.
